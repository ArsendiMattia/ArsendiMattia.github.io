I want to compute $ \nabla^2_{\boldsymbol{\theta}} \log p( \boldsymbol{\theta} \mid \boldsymbol{y}, \boldsymbol{x}) $, where $\boldsymbol{\theta} = (\theta_1, \theta_2, ..., \theta_n)$ and $D$ represents the observed data. 

$$
\begin{align}
  \log p( \boldsymbol{\theta} \mid \boldsymbol{y}, \boldsymbol{x}) 
  &\propto \log p(\boldsymbol{\theta}, \boldsymbol{y}, \boldsymbol{x}) \nonumber \\
  &\propto \log p(\boldsymbol{y} \mid \boldsymbol{\theta}, \boldsymbol{x}) p(\boldsymbol{x}) p(\boldsymbol{\theta}) \nonumber \\
\end{align}
$$
